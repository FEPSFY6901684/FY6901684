Conference Project: The Physical and Logical Limititations of Time Travel

Abstract

Time travel; the hypothetical activity of traversing into the past or future. It is a concept that has entertained the minds of both scientists and non-scientists alike. As our understanding of technology advances, the notion of time travel grows increasingly attractive. Having often appearing in numerous forms of popular-media – but is it just fiction? The answer: No, but with a heavy asterisk involved. Albert Einstein’s proposed theory of relativity, though incomplete, suggests that with suitable conditions; time travel, past and future, is mathematically possible. However, before we even fathom as to how those conditions could be met, there are paradoxical inconsistencies that limit the possibility of time travel, posing questions and threats to the laws of causality and our grasp of physics. This article seeks to describe those limitations, such as the “Grandfather” and “Bootstrap” paradoxes, and review how humanities’ most accurate theories attempt to resolve them.
